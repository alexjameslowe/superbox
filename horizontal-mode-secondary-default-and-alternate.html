Handling the secondary dimension. The idea is this: The primary dimension is the "conserved" one. For horizontal-mode elements, horizontal is the primary dimension and vertical is the secondary. And for vertical-mode elements, it's the reverse. I've gone on at length about how the primary dimension works, but what about the secondary? Where the primary rules are complex, the secondary rules are simple. For horizontal-mode elements, by default, they should take up all the height available.
For vertical-mode element, by default, they should take up all the width available.
